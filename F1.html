What is Formula 1?

Formula 1 is the top tier of international racing for single-seater and open wheeled cars. The competition is sanctioned and by Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA). The FIA Formula 1 World Championship has been and is the premier racing series since its inaugaral season in 1951.

10 teams compete in the year round season with two drivers per team. The teams compete for the World Constructors Championship (WCC) and the individual drivers gunning for the World Drivers Championship (WDC). The driver with the most points by the end of the season wins the WDC whereas the team with the most points (collective of the points of both drivers) wins the WCC.











Race Weekend Format









A 'Race Weekend' runs from Friday to Sunday 5 sessions in total. 3 Free Practice sessions, 1 Qualifying session and the main race or the 'Grand Prix'. The first 2 Free Practice sessions are on Friday with the last Free Practice session and Qualifying on Saturday and the Grand Prix on Sunday.

From 2021, a new weekend format is being tested that involves a short Sprint Race a day before the actual race. In 2021 and 2022, Qualifying determined the starting order for the Sprint and the result of the Sprint Race determined the starting order for the Grand Prix. From 2023, the Sprint races will have their own qualifying session that would determine the starting order for the Sprint and a seperate qualifying to determine the starting order for the Grand Prix.
