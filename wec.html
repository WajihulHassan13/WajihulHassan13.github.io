WEC: A test of stamina and strategy

What is WEC?

The WEC (World Endurance Championship)is one of the most prestigious racing series and the top series for endurance racing and is sanctioned by the FIA.. WEC cars are close-wheeled and single-seater with races with a minimum length of 6 hours and can last upto 24 hours. The most prestigious of these events is the anual 24 Hours of Le Mans held in Le Mans, France every year. The race (along with the F1 Monaco Grand Prix and the Indy 500) is one of if not the most prestigious events in the world. Races are have different types of classes competing to win in their respective classes. The different classes of cars have major differences in both performance and technological complexity.

The inaugral season of the World Endurance Championship took place in 2012 as a replacement for the existing top tier of endurance racing series; the Intercontinental Le Mans Cup. It used a similar format and had 8 races around the world including the infamous 24 Hours of Le Mans

The World Endurance Championship is an enthralling combination of high-speed racing and tactical genius. It tests not just drivers' abilities but also manufacturers' engineering prowess. The WEC continues to captivate motorsport enthusiasts all around the world with legendary races like the 24 Hours of Le Mans and a commitment to technological innovation.
